What's risograph?

In short, it's digital screen printing! The risograph printer looks like a photocopier but works like a screenprinter. Using spot colors and stencils, it creates unique printing results.

"The process is perfect for graphic art (vector based work) typography, and illustration, but also works wonders across painting and photography using variations of CMYK."

-Risotto Studio risottostudio.com

Why I love Risograph Printing

  • It uses soy based inks that are non-toxic.
  • It's an affordable method of printing.
  • Printers are energy efficient (hello sustainability).
  • It looks freaking cool!

How does Riso Work?

  1. The machine uses your uploaded or scanned image to make paper stencil. This stencil wraps around the ink drum. This process is calling making masters
  2. Paper is placed on the feeder and passed through one or two of the ink drums
  3. The machine creates a digital stamp of the individual colors and together they create a colorful, vibrant image.
